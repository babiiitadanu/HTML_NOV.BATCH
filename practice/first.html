COFFEE

Coffee is a globally traded beverage derived from roasted seeds (beans) of cherries grown on Coffea trees, primarily in the tropical "Coffee Belt". Rich in caffeine and antioxidants, it is renowned for boosting energy and providing potential health benefits, including enhanced liver protection and lowered risks of type 2 diabetes and Parkinson's disease. Key Coffee Information:-
Origins: Coffee is thought to have originated in Ethiopia, with early cultivation in Arabia. Varieties: The two main species are Arabica (smoother, higher altitude) and Robusta (stronger, higher caffeine). Production: Brazil is the world's largest producer, followed by Vietnam and Colombia. Consumption: Finland has the highest coffee consumption per capita. Processing: Coffee beans are the seeds of red "cherries" that grow on bushes. Brewing Methods: Common methods include pour-over, espresso, drip, and French press. Health: Moderate coffee consumption may lower the risk of heart disease and depression.

Fun Facts:-
Coffee is the second most traded commodity in the world, following crude oil. It takes approximately 3-4 years for a coffee tree to bear fruit. Decaf coffee still contains a small amount of caffeine

Quick Facts:-
Origins: Most legends trace coffee's discovery to 9th-century Ethiopia, where a goatherd named Kaldi noticed his flock became unusually energetic after eating red berries from a specific bush. Global Leaders: Brazil is the world's leading producer, while Finland has the highest per-capita consumption. The "Bean" Myth: Coffee "beans" are actually the pits or seeds of the coffee cherry fruit. Most Expensive: Kopi Luwak from Indonesia is one of the world's priciest coffees, made from beans that have passed through the digestive tract of an Asian palm civet.
